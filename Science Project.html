India's primary contribution to satellite technology to projects like GSAT is development of advanced communication satellites, used for services like television broadcasting,high- speed internet,telecommunication and data transmission across the country,particularly in remote areas.

GSAT stands for 'Geosynchronous Satellite'.It is made by ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation).

GSAT contributions are:-

1)Broadcasting services:-GSAT satellites are primarily used for television broadcasting, enabling wild reach for television signals across India.

2)Disaster management:-GSAT satellite system can be used for weather forecasting and disaster warning system,aiding in emergency response.

Satellites help with weather prediction, navigation and disaster management in many ways, including:-

1)Weather prediction:-Satellites provide real-time data on whether patterns.So, that we can tell people about wheather, temperature,etc.

2)Navigation:-Satellite provide navigation services.

Disaster management:-Satellites help with disaster management by:-

1)Providing early warnings.

2)Mapping vulnerable areas.

3)Supporting post - disaster relief and recovery.
